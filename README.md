## 📌 Project Overview

This case study examines **Gucci's business model and pricing strategy** through the lens of **Management Economics**. It applies core economic frameworks — Income Elasticity of Demand (YED) and the Veblen Good Effect — to explain how luxury brands sustain demand even as prices rise.

## 🎯 Research Questions

- How does income elasticity shape demand for Gucci products across economic cycles?
- Does Gucci exhibit Veblen Good behavior — where higher prices *increase* demand?
- How does Gucci balance exclusivity with market expansion?
- What makes Gucci's pricing strategy resilient in downturns?

## 📐 Economic Framework

### Income Elasticity of Demand (YED)
```
YED = % Change in Quantity Demanded / % Change in Income
```

- Luxury goods → YED > 1 (Superior Goods)
- Demand rises **disproportionately** with income
- Recessions reduce aspirational buyer spending

### The Veblen Good Effect

| Normal Good | Veblen Good |
|---|---|
| Higher price → Lower demand | Higher price → Higher demand |
| Utility-driven purchase | Status-driven purchase |
| Elastic pricing | Inelastic / reverse-elastic pricing |

> Gucci leverages the Veblen Effect by maintaining deliberately high prices to signal prestige and exclusivity.

## 📊 Key Findings

### Price Elasticity by Product Category

| Product Category | Price Elasticity | Demand Type |
|---|---|---|
| Heritage Leather Goods (Bamboo Bag, GG Monogram) | Highly Inelastic | Veblen Good |
| Trend-Driven Fashion Items | Moderately Elastic | Superior Good |
| Entry-Level Accessories | More Elastic | Aspirational Good |

### Competitive Landscape

| Brand | Strategy | Elasticity Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Gucci | Balance of exclusivity + volume | Moderate Veblen |
| Hermès | Extreme scarcity model | Strong Veblen |
| Louis Vuitton | Broad accessibility | Lower Veblen |
| Chanel | Frequent price increases | Strong Veblen |

## 🏢 Business Model Highlights
```
Gucci's Value Formula:
├── Italian Craftsmanship + Heritage (since 1921)
├── Direct Retail Distribution (no discounting)
├── Controlled Scarcity (limited editions)
├── Heavy Marketing Investment (runway, celebrity, digital)
├── Omnichannel Strategy (boutiques + digital + NFTs)
└── Non-price Competition (innovation + brand storytelling)
```

---

## 💡 Strategic Insights

- **Scarcity = Desirability:** Controlled supply boosts perceived value
- **Price as Identity:** Consumers pay premium to signal wealth and status
- **Brand Dilution Risk:** Over-distribution weakens the Veblen effect
- **Recession Vulnerability:** Aspirational middle-income buyers are the most price-sensitive segment
- **Digital Relevance:** Gen Z and Millennial engagement through social media sustains long-term demand
